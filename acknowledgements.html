Research Mentors - My Guiding Lights

Dr. Utpal Singha Roy
College Teacher & Research Initiator
My heartfelt gratitude to Dr. Utpal Singha Roy, my college teacher, who introduced me to the fascinating world of research and guided me throughout my career. His early mentorship laid the foundation for my research journey and instilled in me a deep passion for scientific inquiry.
Prof. Santanu Ray
PhD Supervisor
I am deeply grateful to Prof. Santanu Ray, my PhD supervisor, whose constant encouragement inspired me to strive for excellence and whose guidance helped me navigate important decisions in life. His wisdom extended far beyond research, and he is always available whenever I seek his counsel. His mentorship has profoundly shaped both my professional and personal growth, and I consider myself truly fortunate to have such an extraordinary guide.
Prof. Young-Seuk Park
Korean Postdoc Supervisor
I owe special thanks to Prof. Young-Seuk Park, my Korean postdoctoral supervisor, who turned my dream of pursuing a foreign postdoc into reality. During my tenure of just 14 months, I gained not only profound knowledge in ecological modelling and informatics but also the privilege of knowing a remarkable human being. In a country where I had no one, he became my guardian, taking care of me beyond academic guidance. He was not merely a postdoctoral mentor; he believed in holistic development ‚Äî nurturing my physical and mental well-being, fostering a positive lab environment, encouraging my hobbies, and guiding my future. I will never forget the countless hours he spent coaching me on how to excel in job interviews, or the day he drove for hours to bid me farewell as I returned to India. His words ‚Äî that he would continue to guide me even from afar ‚Äî remain true, for he still does, despite the distance.
Dr. Anuradha Bhat
Postdoc Mentor at IISER Kolkata
I am deeply thankful to Dr. Anuradha Bhat for offering me a postdoctoral position in India at a time when I was desperately seeking an opportunity. Beyond her invaluable guidance in my research, she generously gave me time and encouragement to prepare for a crucial job interview. In my brief tenure at IISER, I consider myself fortunate to have met a mentor like her ‚Äî someone who is not only an exceptional guide but also a wonderful human being.
