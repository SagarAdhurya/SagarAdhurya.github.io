Indian Grey Wolf Conservation Project

Wildlife Information and Nature Guide Society
May 2024 ‚Äì February 2027
Principal & Co-Principal Investigator
Funding Partners
WWF-India
The Habitat Trust
Wildlife Trust of India
Wildlife Conservation Trust

A comprehensive, multi-year umbrella project integrating field ecology, population modelling, and stakeholder engagement to secure viable wolf populations and reduce human-wildlife conflict across priority landscapes in India.

Key Objectives
  • Population estimation using robust, repeatable protocols and integrated monitoring systems
  • Prey-base assessment through detailed analysis of prey availability and predator-prey dynamics
  • Corridor mapping to identify, prioritize, and protect functional movement pathways
  • Conflict mitigation via tested interventions and community co-existence frameworks

Doctoral Research Project

Visva-Bharati University
2016 ‚Äì 2022
UGC NET-JRF/SRF

Guanotrophication by aquatic avifauna on freshwater ecosystem dynamics

Supervisor: Prof. Santanu Ray

Laboratory: Systems Ecology and Ecological Modelling Laboratory

Process-based modelling of phosphorus cycling influenced by waterbirds with scenario analysis and ecosystem diagnostics for shallow tropical freshwater systems.

Key Outcomes
  • First process based model for guanotrophication and pioneering work on guanotrophy in Asia
  • In absence of other source of nutrient, waterbirds can be major source of nutrient as found in the studied lake
  • The impact of guanotrophy in the studied lake is gradual not instant, which mostly affect the abiotic state variables
  • The dynamics of the studied lake is mainly regulated by temperature mediated internal cycling

Machine learning applications in fish community analysis
