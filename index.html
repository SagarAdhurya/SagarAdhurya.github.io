About Me

Hello, I am Dr. Sagar Adhurya – a zoologist, ecologist, and teacher with a lifelong curiosity about how living systems work. My academic journey has taken me from earning a PhD in Zoology at Visva-Bharati University to working as a postdoctoral researcher at Kyung Hee University in South Korea and at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Kolkata. These experiences shaped my research in ecological modelling, biodiversity, conservation, and the use of machine learning to understand ecological patterns. At present, I serve as an Assistant Professor of Zoology, where I enjoy teaching, mentoring, and exploring new research ideas with students.

My work focuses on process-based models, food web dynamics, ecological succession, and species conservation, with the broader aim of connecting theory with real-world applications. Over the years, I have published research articles, contributed a book chapter, reviewed for international journals, and been fortunate to receive recognition such as awards from the International Society for Ecological Modelling and a WWF-India conservation grant for my project on the Indian Grey Wolf. Outside the classroom and lab, I find joy in birdwatching, wildlife photography, animal call recording, and trekking. I am also a founder member of the Wildlife Information and Nature Guide Society (WINGS), where I have taken on leadership roles to support community-driven conservation. Through this website, I hope to share my research, conservation initiatives, field experiences, and teaching journey, while also inspiring others who are passionate about nature and ecology.
